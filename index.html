Biography

Steve Jobs (1955-2011) was a visionary entrepreneur and co-founder of Apple Inc. His life and work revolutionized the tech industry. Born in San Francisco, he dropped out of college but continued his education through unconventional means. In 1976, he, along with Steve Wozniak and Ronald Wayne, founded Apple in his garage. His innovative leadership led to iconic products like the Macintosh, iPod, iPhone, and iPad, reshaping how we interact with technology. Jobs was known for his obsessive attention to design and detail, emphasizing user experience. His return to Apple in 1997 saw the company's remarkable turnaround. He blended technology with the arts, creating products that were both functional and beautiful. His "Think Different" approach inspired a generation of creatives and entrepreneurs. Despite his successes, Jobs faced setbacks and controversies, but his persistence and determination remained. He passed away in 2011, leaving behind a legacy that continues to influence modern technology and design, making him an enduring figure in the world of innovation.
